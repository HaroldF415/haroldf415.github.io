Updating Portfolio.

Posted on: January 29, 2019

Every time I start programming again it all comes back to me faster than it did the last time. I think this is a good thing. Going over my code again and again has made me realize most of what I have in here is copied and pasted from other parts of my portfolio. There are some mistakes in each individual blog that I have not picked up on. Tyring to make the site more responsive can be a big task for me but since I have free time on my hands I'm willing to put in the work. The first thing to do is to go over every post and find out what is wrong in each one. Yesterday I managed to successfully updated the skeleton part of the blog entry navigation. I have yet to decide on how I should present it to the user. Should really start watching UX videos. As of now this site has grown without any sort of plans. I've been adding little bits and pieces to this from day one. Trying to take a more holistic approach to this website. This does not mean I will start from scratch but have a more controlled aspect when changing things around and not just change things for the fun of it. We shall start with the blog.html page.

blog.html

This page has a stack of blog entries dating back to 08/12/15. I have spent the majority of those years going back and forth with programming. You'd think I'd be an expert by now. From the moment I lay eyes on this page something seems off: the font, the colors, or the style of it. Although the style is the only cool thing I like about this. I don't quite remember how I came up with it. I just like it.

  • Each blog entry on this page is inside a div called: blog_main_container.
  • Then the blog_main_container contains a section called: blog_entries_preview. These contain a small picture that sort of describes what is inside said post.

There are more things that each blog entry contains and I will go into more detail on the next post. My time has run out for today. Between fixing the site and actually writing down a post the time has flown by.
