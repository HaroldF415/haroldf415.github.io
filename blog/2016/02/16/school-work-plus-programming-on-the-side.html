Trying to find a balance between college studies and programming.

Posted on: February16, 2016

In an effort to keep track of my progress as a Full Stack Web Developer I decided to write code at least 2hrs per day. The rest will be focused on school work.

It's constant war in my head. One side wants to program as much as possible before graduating college and the other is trying to keep my grades up so when I do graduate from college I don't end up with a bad GPA. Which currently is not looking so good.

I will not turn into a political blog or anything but the American Education System really does suck. I understand that in college they try to make you an educated person all around but when will I need Art History 101!?!?!

Well that's what I wanted to get out of my system. It is no joke because my time to graduate is soon approaching and I have no leads on any internships. It also doesn't help that it is a requirement that you have at least one intership under your belt before you graduate.

Like I said I will be trying to touch code at least 2 - 3 hrs per day.

Over the holiday (President's Day) weekend I managed to put together the free online classes for programing I'm following and added a few more to the mix

  • CodeCademy
  • Udemy
  • Udacity
  • The Odin Project
  • FreeCodeCamp
  • TreeHouse

I started learning to program back in the day with CodeCademy so it feels right to finish that course first. Obviously I will finish the free content first and then move on to the payment plan which offers quizzes and more projects. I do not intend to start paying now as it feels like a waste.

Why you ask? Well simply put I'm back in school for the Spring 2016, attend a work study internship (not a programming internship) and help out at home and honestly I can't squeeze in hours of programing into my schedule let alone pay for them. So yeah when I have more time to spare I will start paying for these services.

Also before I forget this 2hrs of programing will include committing something to GitHub everyday. To have a very nice streak going and learn something in the process.
